Do You Prefer Eating at Home or Eating Out?

About this Episode

🎙️ Welcome to English with me! — the podcast that helps you learn real English, naturally. In this episode, Rebecca and Mike explore the age-old question: is it better to cook at home or eat out? They discuss the "therapeutic" nature of cooking for some, the lack of "bandwidth" for others after a long day, and how inflation has caused restaurant prices to "go through the roof."

Vocabulary & Keywords

Word / Phrase Definition Example
Therapeutic Having a good effect on the body or mind; contributing to a sense of well-being or relaxation. "Mike finds chopping vegetables to be quite therapeutic after a stressful day."
Bandwidth The mental or emotional capacity needed to handle a task or situation. "After a long day of meetings, Rebecca often feels she doesn't have the bandwidth to decide what to cook."
Leftovers Food remaining from a previous meal. "One of the best parts of making a large Sunday roast is having enough leftovers for Monday's lunch."
From scratch To make something from the very beginning using basic ingredients rather than pre-made mixes or components. "Making bread from scratch takes time, but the smell of fresh loaves in the oven is worth it."
Gone through the roof To increase rapidly and reach an extremely high level. "Since the new taxes were introduced, the price of imported coffee has gone through the roof."

Practice Questions

  • Why does Mike consider cooking to be a "therapeutic" activity?
  • What does Rebecca mean when she says she lacks the "bandwidth" to cook on weekdays?
  • According to the discussion, how have external factors like inflation affected the decision to eat out?
